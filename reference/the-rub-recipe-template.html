Grilling

Dry-rub grilled wings

High heat, one heavy coat of rub, and the patience not to check them every 90 seconds. That's the whole trick.

10 minPrep
20 minCook
4Servings
EasyDifficulty
Shop the rub →
The story

We built this one on a Tuesday because we didn't want to wait for the weekend. No marinade, no brine, just a heavy hand with the rub and a hot grill. It became the office lunch every time someone needed to test a new batch — if the wings disappeared in ten minutes, the batch was good.

Ingredients

For the wings

  • 2 lb chicken wings, split
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil
  • Kosher salt, to taste

For the rub

  • 3 tbsp The Rub
  • 1 tbsp melted butter, for tossing

This recipe leans on our original blend — the one from the deli tub. One pouch covers about six batches.

Shop the rub →

Directions

Dry the wings

Pat wings completely dry, then toss with baking powder and a pinch of salt. Rest uncovered in the fridge for at least an hour — this is what gets the skin crispy without a deep fryer.

Coat heavy

Toss the wings with oil, then coat generously with the rub. Heavier than feels reasonable. You want color, not a light dusting.

Grill hot

Grill over direct high heat, turning every few minutes, until the skin is charred in spots and an instant-read thermometer hits 165°F. About 18–20 minutes total.

Toss and rest

Toss hot wings with melted butter and a final light hit of the rub. Let them rest 5 minutes before serving — they're never as good straight off the grill as they are five minutes later.

Pro tip

Two-zone fire if you've got one. Sear over direct heat for color, finish over indirect so the rub doesn't burn before the meat's done.
